## Project Report (Key Findings & Recommendations)

### Finding 1:
- The attrition rate of 16.12% exceeds the healthy range, which is typically below 10%.

### Recommendation 1:
- During the hiring process, prioritize candidates with marital statuses other than single.

### Finding 2:
- Employees in the research director job role exhibit the highest attrition rate at 23.75%. Analysis of attendance data reveals a correlation with leaving late hours, indicating a significant factor in attrition.

### Recommendation 2:
- Implement measures to reduce overtime for employees.
- Conduct discussions with research directors to identify any underlying issues contributing to attrition.

### Finding 3:
- Despite having fewer employees, the human resources department experiences a double attrition rate (30.16%) compared to other departments. Analysis suggests a correlation with high average late departure hours.

### Recommendation 3:
- Implement measures to reduce overtime for HR employees.
- Conduct discussions with HR staff to uncover any additional factors contributing to attrition.

### Finding 4:
- There is a positive correlation between the frequency of work-related travel and attrition rate.

### Recommendation 4:
- Limit employee travel to essential purposes only.
- Provide attractive travel allowances to compensate employees.

### Finding 5:
- There is a positive correlation between younger age and attrition rate.

### Recommendation 5:
- Offer competitive salaries for both junior and senior employees.
- Provide enhanced medical insurance and benefits specifically tailored for younger employees.
